The Problem

Leads were coming in.
Nobody was picking up.

When I joined the company, the sales team had a name for a pattern they'd accepted as normal: CNR — Call No Response. Leads would fill in a form, a rep would call, and... nothing. Voicemail. Silence. The lead would cool off, the rep would move on, and another potential enrolment would evaporate.

The frustrating thing wasn't that leads were low quality. It was that nobody had stopped to ask why nobody was picking up — or what we could change on our end to fix it.

So I got curious.

"The leads weren't the problem. The system around them was."

I started looking at the full journey from the moment someone submitted a form to the moment Sales made contact. What I found was a chain of small, fixable failures — each one individually manageable, but together creating a pipeline that was leaking at every joint.

What I found
The Diagnosis

Three broken links
in the same chain.

Before — What Was Broken
  • Form asked "what time suits you?" with a vague range — leads picked broadly, reps called at the wrong moment
  • Follow-up was weak and inconsistent — a call attempt, maybe two, then the lead was deprioritized
  • Reps had no context going into calls — no idea what course, what concern, what life situation
  • Sales were juggling who to call, when to call, and what to say — all at once, manually
  • The lead had no sense of commitment — they'd filled in a form, not made an appointment
After — What I Built
  • Specific time slots on the form: 9–10am, 10–11am — so calls were expected, not random
  • Multi-channel follow-up: automated WhatsApp + email reminders confirming the slot
  • Smart segmentation in HubSpot capturing course interest, motivation, and objection type
  • Reps wake up with a clear queue: who to call, when, what course, what barrier to address
  • Calendly link in the sequence — the lead commits by clicking yes, not just by filling a form
The build
The Build

I didn't overhaul everything.
I fixed one thing at a time.

The temptation in a situation like this is to redesign everything at once. I didn't do that. I started with the thing that seemed smallest but was actually the root of everything else: the form.

01
Changed the form — specific slots, not open ranges

Instead of asking "what time is suitable for you?" with a vague morning/afternoon option, I changed it to specific slots: 9am–10am, 10am–11am, and so on. Suddenly, when a rep called, the lead was expecting it. Pick-up rates improved immediately. This was the unlock that made everything else possible.

02
Built multi-channel appointment reminders

I noticed something obvious once I started looking: people get WhatsApp messages and emails for dental appointments, GP visits, restaurant bookings. Why weren't we doing the same? I implemented automated reminders — WhatsApp and email — confirming the slot. I piloted it for one faculty first. No-show rates dropped. I rolled it out.

03
Built intelligence into the form itself

I wasn't satisfied with just getting people to pick up. I wanted Sales to walk into every call already knowing three things: what course the lead was interested in, whether that interest had changed, and what was actually stopping them from enrolling — time, money, or commitment. So I redesigned the form to capture exactly that, feeding it directly into HubSpot contact records.

04
Automated the sales queue entirely

With clean data in HubSpot, I built workflows that did the prioritization for reps automatically. No more juggling. No more "who do I call first?" Every morning, the queue is built for them: name, slot, course, objection type. The rep's job became consultation, not logistics.

05
Reframed the call as a consultation, not a sales call

The final piece was psychological. By the time the rep calls, the lead has confirmed their slot via WhatsApp, clicked a Calendly link, and answered questions about their goals and concerns. They've made micro-commitments. They're not receiving a cold sales call — they're attending a consultation they signed up for. That changes everything about how the conversation starts.

The thinking behind it
The Insight

The real problem was never
the leads. It was the ask.

Most lead follow-up systems are designed around the convenience of the sales team. Call when you're free. Follow up when you remember. Pitch the course you think they want.

What I built was designed around the psychology of the lead. People don't pick up unexpected calls. People don't commit to vague asks. People don't respond well to a stranger who knows nothing about them launching into a pitch.

The Core Insight

Commitment starts before the call. If someone has confirmed a slot, clicked a reminder, and answered questions about their goals — they're not receiving a sales call. They're arriving at a conversation they chose to have. That one shift, from reactive cold call to expected consultation, is what made the whole system work.

The automation didn't replace the human element. It protected it — so that by the time a real person picked up the phone, the groundwork was already done. The lead was warm. The rep was informed. The conversation could actually go somewhere.

What it became
The Outcome

50% fewer manual tasks.
100% of the human moments, preserved.

The system I built reduced manual sales tasks by 50%. Reps no longer needed to decide who to call, when to call, or what to say — the workflow handled that. What they got back was time and clarity: time to have better conversations, and clarity on exactly who needed what.

The wider email programme I built on top of this infrastructure achieved a 36% open rate on a 9,000+ contact campaign — generating 5 direct sales from a single send during a period (December) when most teams accept low engagement as seasonal fact.

The system has since been rolled out across 4 brands at the company. Every campaign since joining has generated revenue. Not "contributed to pipeline." Generated revenue.

"The goal was never to remove people from the process. It was to make sure that when people showed up, the process was actually ready for them."

What started as a question — why isn't anyone picking up? — became a fully automated lead qualification and handoff system that treats every contact as an individual, every sales rep as a consultant, and every interaction as an opportunity to earn the next one.
